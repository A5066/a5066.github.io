About the Temple

Jeenbhawani Maa Dhaam, located in the Aravalli hills of Sikar district, Rajasthan, is a revered 12th-century temple built during the reign of Prithviraj Chauhan. Dedicated to Goddess Jeenbhawani, an avatar of Goddess Durga, it is a place of miracles and devotion. The temple is known for having the longest song among Rajasthan's folk deities and has withstood historical challenges, including attempts by Aurangzeb to destroy it. Devotees believe every wish sought here is fulfilled swiftly.

Legends of the Divine Place

This sacred site holds profound historical significance dating back to the Mahabharata era. After losing their kingdom in the infamous dice game, the Pandavas sought refuge here during their exile. The divine energy of this place provided them solace and strength during their most challenging period.

Centuries later, during Aurangzeb's reign, the Mughal emperor attempted to destroy Harsh Bhairav's temple and persecute its worshippers. As priests and devotees prayed desperately to Jeen Mata for protection, the goddess manifested her divine power. She sent swarms of honeybees to attack Aurangzeb's forces, punishing them for their sacrilege. This miraculous intervention earned her the title Bhawrawali Maa (Goddess of Honeybees).

Defeated and humbled by this divine display, Aurangzeb not only abandoned his destructive campaign but also visited Jeen Mata's temple in reverence. As an act of atonement, he donated a golden chhatra (a ceremonial canopy) that remains a sacred artifact in the temple to this day.

These legends testify to the enduring spiritual power of this sacred site, where divine intervention has repeatedly protected the faithful throughout history.

Origin of the Devi

According to sacred legends, approximately 1200 years ago, King Gangosingh Chauhan - a devoted worshipper of Maa Durga - received a divine promise that the Goddess would incarnate in his family lineage and be worshipped by devotees during Kaliyuga.

During a visit to Lohargal, the king encountered a celestial being whose extraordinary beauty captivated him. He proposed marriage, to which she agreed under one condition: the king must always knock before entering her chambers. The king accepted this condition and they were wed, eventually bearing two children - Jeen Mata and Harsh Bhairav.

One fateful day, the king forgot this sacred agreement and entered unannounced, witnessing his queen in her true divine form as a lioness. Terrified by this revelation and having broken their covenant, the celestial queen returned to the heavens, leaving behind her mortal family.

After King Gangosingh's passing, the siblings grew up together under Harsh Bhairav's loving care. When Harsh eventually married, his wife grew jealous of the profound bond between brother and sister. During a traditional Rajput festival where brothers gift ornaments to their sisters, Harsh's wife manipulated events to prove she was his favorite by receiving gifts first - despite Harsh's true devotion to his sister.

Heartbroken by this betrayal, Jeen Mata retreated to the mountains where her tears of kajal (eye makeup) stained the cliffs black, giving the sacred site its name - Kajal Shikhar (Mountain of Kajal). Though Harsh searched for his sister and begged her return, Jeen Mata remained devoted to her spiritual practice, ultimately attaining divine form as Jeen Bhawani while Harsh became Harsh Bhairav Dada. The jealous sister-in-law was transformed into a lizard by Jeen Mata's divine curse.

This sacred origin story explains the eternal bond between Jeen Mata and Harsh Bhairav Dada, and why devotees continue to worship them with profound reverence at their holy shrines.
